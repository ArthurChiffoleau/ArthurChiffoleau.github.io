From making an economy to a controller

Hermes

[Work in progress]

I am currently working on Hermes, it's a plane game in wich you explore the world and you try escape a mysterious cataclysm. You fly from island to island while trying to understand the origin of cataclysm and maybe fight back.

I am working as Game Designer and Level designer, if at this time my main jobs is to think/write documentation about different feature related to the plane, tweak settings of the plane and doing part of the level design.

Economy

At first there was an economic part in the game, the player was a merchant that would land on an island and will buy and sell merchandises. I was in charge of designing the economic system to create a fun and engaging merchant experience that would complement the fly experience.

  • Chercher quel sont nos spécification
  • Création d'un prototype
  • Premier lot de problèmes (Stagnation, intérêt de vente)
  • Comment les résoudre
  • Chercher des référence, comment marche les système
  • Première mise en place des systèmes
  • Nouvelle recherche des concept économique

A big change

In the end we decided to cut the whole economic part, the main reason was time. We are doing this as part of school project and will have jury that will play our for a maximum of 30 minutes. Making a plane alongside a merchant game will be very hard because learning the merchant take quite some time and there no point in making an hypothetic experience that will never be play.

By making this hard decision we will be hopefully able to make a better plane game that will deliver a strong experience.
